## The Pedagogical Gap
*   Current curricula often focus on tool-specific training
*   Need for integrated workflows aligned with real-world production
*   **The Goal:** Balance creative exploration with technical rigor

---

## The Industry-Aligned Platform
*   **The Stage:** 32′×16′ LED wall (2.3 mm pixel pitch)
*   **The Tech:** Dual NVIDIA A6000 GPUs + Brompton processors
*   **The Brain:** Unreal Engine for real-time rendering
*   **The Sync:** Mo-Sys tracking for physical/virtual camera alignment

---

## A Heterogeneous Cohort
*   Students from Film, Animation, Game Design, and Digital Media
*   Minimal prerequisites required
*   Focus on multi-role collaboration and shared vocabulary

---

## Three Learning Environments
1.  **Screening Room:** Conceptual critique and shot planning
2.  **Media Lab:** Digital asset creation and version control
3.  **LED Stage:** Full hardware integration and live testing

---

## Course Progression
*   **Weeks 1-2:** System-level intro & embodied activities
*   **Weeks 3-5:** Concept development & team formation
*   **Weeks 6+:** Iterative testing and role rotation

---

## Assessment Milestones
*   **Storyboard:** Narrative flow and staging intent
*   **Previsualization:** Rolling submissions of shot drafts
*   **Technical Visualization:** Toolchains, assets, and stage readiness

---

## Key Takeaways
*   **Ecosystem Fluency:** Understanding how creative decisions affect technical constraints
*   **Workflow Literacy:** Practical experience with Unreal Engine, Blender, and Perforce
*   **The Hidden Lesson:** The critical importance of IT infrastructure and logistics
